## n8n-automation: Infrastructure & Documentation Wins

Most of my focus this week landed on the `n8n-automation` project. I finally got around to something I've been meaning to do: properly defining and deploying our n8n stack using infrastructure-as-code. It's so satisfying to not just spin something up manually, but to have it codified and repeatable. Part of that process also led me to hunt down and remove some long-forgotten, unused AWS Lambdas. Feels good to declutter the AWS account a bit – less cruft, less to worry about.

The real heavy lifting, surprisingly, wasn't just the `bin/app.ts` changes, but the documentation. Diving deep into the `README.md` and creating a brand-new `QUICKSTART.md` took a significant chunk of time. But honestly, it's always worth it. Documenting how to deploy and get started is crucial, not just for others, but for future me who will inevitably forget the nuances.
